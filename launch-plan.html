Trust Video Script — Record This Today
Action Required

What This Is

A 60-second video sent to every person who registers. It goes out via WhatsApp the day after they pay. Its only job is to make them show up on April 18.

Why This Matters

Confirmation videos increase show-up rates by 25-40% over email-only reminders. People who see your face after paying feel a personal connection. They don't skip something that feels personal.

How to Film

  • Phone, horizontal, propped up on something stable
  • Your office or anywhere that looks like a real CISO's environment
  • Natural light from a window if possible
  • One take. Don't memorize the script — just hit the beats
  • Imperfections are fine. Over-produced = corporate = no trust
  • 60 seconds. Not 30 (too short). Not 2 minutes (they won't finish).

The Script

Read it once. Then just talk. One sentence at a time. Every line ties back to THEIR career, not yours. 60 seconds total.

Hey. You just grabbed a seat for the Career Briefing. Good move.

[2 sec — look at camera, small nod, no pause]

Most people in your position spend months looking at certifications, reading job posts, watching YouTube — trying to figure out the path on their own. You just skipped all of that.

[About THEM. Not about you. Don't add credentials here — resist the urge.]

Here's what you're walking into on April 18.

[Transition line. Say it, move on. No pause.]

I've been hiring cybersecurity professionals for 28 years. Deloitte. EY. Shell. And the number one reason IT people stay stuck is not skill. It's how they position themselves. That's what I'm going to fix for you.

[STOP here. Don't add the wire fraud story, the Qatar bank, the CIO award. One credential line. "That's what I'm going to fix for YOU" — pulls it back to them.]

I'm going to pull up someone's LinkedIn live and show the room exactly what I look for when I'm on the hiring side. You'll see what gets you noticed — and what gets you skipped.

[This is the hook. The thing they can't get anywhere else. Say it like you're telling a friend what's going to happen — not presenting.]

Block 11 AM Eastern on Saturday the 18th. Set a phone alarm right now while you're watching this. There's no recording — what happens in the room stays in the room.

[Direct instruction. "Right now while you're watching this" = micro-commitment. They physically do something.]

You made a real move today. Most people just think about it. See you on the 18th.

[End. Don't add "if you have any questions..." Don't trail off. "See you on the 18th" = done. Stop talking.]

Delivery Notes (Critical)

No long pauses. If you need to think, stop recording and restart. A pause on video reads as uncertainty. On WhatsApp, people skip ahead or close the video.

One sentence at a time. Say the sentence. Stop. Say the next one. Your natural instinct is to connect ideas with "and also..." and "what I mean by that is..." — don't. Short. Punchy. Move on.

Every line is about THEM. You'll want to talk about your experience. You have ONE credential line. After that, everything is "you" / "your career" / "your LinkedIn." If a sentence starts with "I" more than twice in a row, you've drifted.

60 seconds max. Time yourself. If it's over 75 seconds, you're adding words. Cut, don't add. The viewer is on their phone between meetings.

Don't explain the script, just say it. No "so basically what we're going to do is..." — that's filler. The script already says what you're going to do. Trust the words.

Do NOT

  • Add music, intros, or "hey guys" energy
  • Re-sell the briefing — they already paid. Re-selling creates doubt.
  • Say "thank you so much for registering" — grateful energy lowers authority
  • List the 5 topics or the full agenda — kills curiosity
  • Mention the $4,997 program, the Accelerator, or anything about buying more — this video is about SHOWING UP, not selling
  • Trail off at the end with "so yeah... looking forward to it..." — end clean
