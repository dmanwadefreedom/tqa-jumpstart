Design Notes for Slide Builder

Tone: Corporate briefing. Think McKinsey, not marketing webinar.

Color palette: Dark navy or charcoal background. White and light gray text. One accent color (steel blue or teal) used sparingly.

Typography: Clean sans-serif. Headings in medium weight, not bold. No ALL CAPS except the title slide.

Imagery: Minimal. No stock photos of people at computers. No padlock icons. Data visualizations or clean diagrams only.

Animations: None. Simple cuts or subtle fades.

What to avoid: Exclamation marks, words like "amazing/incredible/game-changing/transform/unleash," emoji of any kind, testimonial slides, pricing/bonuses/stacking, "limited spots" language, anything that looks like an infomercial.

What this deck should feel like: A senior executive walked into a room, shared exactly what he knows, answered questions honestly, and mentioned at the end that he runs a program if anyone wants help. That's it. The authority of the content IS the selling mechanism.
